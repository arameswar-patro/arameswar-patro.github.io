About Me

Welcome to my academic webpage. I am a mathematics student with a deep focus on abstract algebra, representation theory, and algebraic geometry. I am highly motivated by the interplay between combinatorics, geometry, and group theory.

My current studies primarily revolve around understanding the structural properties of algebraic groups and their invariants. I am currently preparing for doctoral studies where I hope to further investigate these areas.

Research Interests

My core mathematical interests lie in the following domains:

  • Coxeter Groups: Exploring their combinatorial aspects, Bruhat order, and applications to representation theory.
  • Simple Groups of Lie Type: Studying their structural properties, root systems, and representations over finite fields.
  • Geometric Invariant Theory: Understanding quotients of algebraic varieties by group actions and stability conditions.
  • Word Maps on Groups: Investigating the image and fibers of word maps on finite simple groups and algebraic groups.

Selected Projects & Seminar Talks

An Introduction to Coxeter Groups and Reflection Groups

Combinatorics Group Theory

A comprehensive reading project focusing on the geometric realization of Coxeter groups as reflection groups. The project covers root systems, the exchange condition, and the classification of finite Coxeter groups.

Word Maps on Finite Simple Groups

Algebra Waring's Problem

An exploratory study on the surjectivity of word maps for specific families of finite simple groups, heavily relying on character theory and probabilistic methods. Explored classical results like the Ore conjecture.

Fundamentals of Geometric Invariant Theory (GIT)

Algebraic Geometry

Seminar talk summarizing the foundational concepts of Mumford's GIT, specifically focusing on affine and projective quotients, and the Hilbert-Mumford criterion for stability.
